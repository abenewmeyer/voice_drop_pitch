Where churn is actually coming from

Based on VoiceDrop's market — real estate, financial services, and insurance — there are four failure points where customers disengage. The methodology to address each one is what you'd be engaging me to build. Click each to see the diagnostic framing.

Problem 01
Silent disengagement before the decision
Customers don't cancel the day they lose faith. They go quiet weeks earlier — and most CS teams have no system to catch it.
Problem 02
Cancellation conversations with no playbook
When a customer says "I want to cancel," the response is improvised. Improvised conversations lose deals that structure would have saved.
Problem 03
Revenue left on the table with power users
Your highest-engagement customers are signaling expansion readiness. Without a trigger-based upsell process, that signal expires unread.
Problem 04
Enterprise accounts managed like standard ones
A high-value account churning carries 10x the revenue impact of a standard account. It rarely gets 10x the attention until it's too late.

What I'd diagnose

Enterprise accounts in regulated industries — the financial services and real estate firms that are VoiceDrop's highest-value customers — churn for reasons that are often political before they're operational. A personnel change, a compliance review, or a vendor audit can put a healthy account at risk overnight. Standard CS response protocols are too slow and too generic for accounts where one cancellation can mean five figures of ARR. The protocol needs to be asymmetric — faster, higher-touch, with different authority levels.
